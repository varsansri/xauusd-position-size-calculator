XAUUSD Lot Size Guide — What is 1 Lot in Gold?

Confused about lot sizes when trading gold? You're not alone. XAUUSD lot sizes work differently from currency pairs, and understanding the difference between standard, mini, and micro lots is essential before placing your first gold trade.

In XAUUSD, 1 standard lot = 100 troy ounces of gold. If gold is trading at $2,000, then 1 standard lot controls $200,000 worth of gold. This is why most retail traders use mini lots (0.1) or micro lots (0.01).

Which Lot Size is Right for Your Account?

  • Under $500: Micro lots only (0.01–0.05). Never risk more than $5 per trade.
  • $500–$2,000: Mini lots (0.01–0.15). Keep risk per trade under $20.
  • $2,000–$10,000: Mini to half-standard (0.1–0.5). 1% risk = $20–$100 per trade.
  • $10,000+: Standard lots appropriate. 1% risk allows $100+ per trade.
Rule of thumb: Your stop loss in dollars should never exceed 2% of your account. Use the position size calculator to confirm your lot size before every trade — not after you enter.

XAUUSD vs Forex Pairs — Key Differences

Unlike EUR/USD where 1 pip = $10 per standard lot, XAUUSD has a different pip value because gold prices are in the hundreds/thousands. The math is simpler in gold: multiply your lot size (in oz) by the pip distance (in $). That's your profit or loss.

Gold also has wider spreads (typically $0.25–$0.50 at major brokers) compared to major currency pairs, so factor this into your stop loss calculations.
